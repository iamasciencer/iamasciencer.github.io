Why astronomy?

I have always been interested in science (as far as i can remember) and was a very curious kid. Especially physics and the stars have fascinated me in a special way. So I decided to study astronomy. Here, I want to give you a little overview of my interests and work in this field.

Chemically Peculiar stars

What are they and why are they here?

The chemically peculiar (CP) stars are stars of spectral type B-F that show various differences in their spectra when compared to "normal" stars in this temperature domain. Depending on what subtype one looks at, different physical processes, from diffusion to magnetic fields, are responsible for the "odd" behaviour of their spectra.

Fig 1: Example of a spectrum of a CP star, specifically of spectral type kA0hA2mA5 SrSiEu. In the lower panel we can see a flux depression (red horizontal line) that is a tell-tale sign of a (strong) magnetic field.

One especially captivating (and poorly researched) question is: When do these peculiarities begin to show? So, we need to try and find pre-main-sequence (PMS) stars that already show signs of being a CP star. In my first paper, we describe nine stars that are likely PMS CP stars. However, there is still little to nothing known about this topic and I want to try and solve this mystery.
